About Me

What I’m good at?

Molecular biology

After a bachelors degree in biology from the Maastricht Science program, I pursued an MSc in Plant Biotechnology at Wageningen University. Through my thesis and internship, I honed my skills in molecular biology and used new biotechnologies such as CRISPR/Cas9 and TurboID biotin-based proximity labeling.

Languages

Half French, half American (USA), I am native in both English and French. During high-school, I got into the American Section of the Option Internationale du Baccalauréat at Lycée Nelson Mandela in Nantes. I also did an internship in Santiago, Chile which allowed me to learn Spanish.

Coding

Ever since COVID, I started my coding journey using R, Java, JavaScript, and later Python. By clicking, you can check one of my group projects where we made an interactive website to visualize gene networks related to schizophrenia and bipolar disorder using wikidata SPARQL querries, the stringdb database, and cytoscape.js.

LbCas12-mediated multiplex gene editing and 2-fluoroadenine counter-selection in Phytophthora palmivora

"CRISPR-Cas systems have moved forward genetic engineering in virtually any organism amenable to genetic modification. In particular, these systems have unlocked unprecedented possibilities to generate mutants in oomycetes, a group of filamentous microbes comprising over two hundred Phytophthora species, including the cacao killer Phytophthora palmivora. Here, we showcase multiplex gene editing in P. palmivora using LbCas12."
